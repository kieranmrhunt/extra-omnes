The Last Veto

Leo XIII is dead after twenty-five years. Sixty-two cardinals enter the Sistine Chapel; sixty-one received their red hats from him. In a Kraków cardinal’s pocket lies an emperor’s veto the Holy See has never recognised. Forty-two votes make a pope.
